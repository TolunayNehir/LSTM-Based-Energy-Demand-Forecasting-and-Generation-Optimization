# LSTM-Based-Energy-Demand-Forecasting-and-Generation-Optimization
Dynamic Energy Demand And Generation Modeling In Smart Cities Using Artificial Neural Networks Paper Repo



**Abstract**


Within the context of modern urbanization and energy consumption, large-scale systems—including sub-sectors such as transportation networks and raw material volumes—are managed. In this context, these systems create complexity because traditional economic models fall short in explaining them. Smart cities are not merely technological infrastructures; they are microeconomic systems built around the challenge of efficiently allocating scarce resources such as budget, energy, and time. The primary objective of this study is to design a comprehensive analysis system by proposing a holistic economic model that utilizes Artificial Neural Networks (ANNs) within smart city ecosystems. The study is based on annual data covering the period from 1980 to 2025, and the methodology consists of two stages: Demand Modeling and Economic Optimization. Demand Modeling: To capture the nonlinear relationships in energy demand, an ANN—specifically the RNN-based Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) architecture, which performs exceptionally well with time-series data—will be employed. Economic Optimization: Various optimization techniques will be applied to ensure that the forecasted production meets demand within budget constraints. The proposed model is expected to detect dynamic changes in demand levels within smart city infrastructures and, within this scope, perform systematic production forecasting. In evaluating the findings, the study estimates energy production levels by analyzing fluctuations in demand and the potential capacity within this context. Additionally, the study aims to provide insights into economies of scale. Furthermore, relevant projections are made by analyzing demand fluctuations and cost changes, as well as technological efficiency and allocation strategies.
Keywords: Artificial Neural Networks, Long Short-Term Memory, Smart Cities, Economies of Scale, Energy Demand Forecasting

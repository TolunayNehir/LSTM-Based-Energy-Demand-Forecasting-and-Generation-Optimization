# LSTM-Based-Energy-Demand-Forecasting-and-Generation-Optimization
Modeling Dynamic Energy Demand and Generation in Smart Cities Using Artificial Neural Networks (1980–2025) Paper Repo



**Abstract**


This study presents a comprehensive economic model based on Artificial Neural Networks (ANNs) aimed at optimizing resource management in smart city ecosystems. The scope of the study encompasses complex energy systems—which traditional economic models struggle to explain in modern urbanization processes—and the microeconomic effects of these systems on budget, time, and energy allocation. The primary objective of the study is to develop a decision-support mechanism capable of systematically identifying dynamic demand fluctuations in smart city infrastructures and providing production forecasts. Large-scale data spanning the 1980–2025 period is analyzed in two stages using demand modeling and economic optimization methods. In the first stage, a Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) network—a deep learning architecture—was employed to model the nonlinear and complex time-series relationships in energy demand. In the second stage, economic optimization techniques were applied to align the obtained demand forecasts with budget constraints and resource efficiency. The study’s findings accurately predict energy production levels. This model also provides forward-looking strategic projections regarding technological efficiency, economies of scale, and resource allocation strategies.



**Keywords:** Artificial Neural Networks, Long Short-Term Memory, Smart Cities, Economies of Scale, Energy Demand Forecasting
